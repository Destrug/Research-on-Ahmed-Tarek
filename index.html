Abstract

This study examines Ahmed Tarek, a newly documented non-binary mammal exhibiting pig-like morphology but with significantly worse hygienic and behavioral traits than common swine (Sus scrofa domesticus). Through observational analysis, we conclude that Ahmed Tarek represents an extreme case of filth adaptation, surpassing even pigs in uncleanliness and disordered lifestyle.

1. Introduction

Pigs (Suidae) are often stereotyped as unclean animals, yet their natural behaviors (e.g., mud-wallowing) serve thermoregulatory and parasitic defense purposes (Bracke, 2019[1]). In contrast, Ahmed Tarek displays no such evolutionary justification for its poor hygiene, suggesting a pathological deviation from biological norms.

Research Question:
Is Ahmed Tarek's lifestyle more unsanitary and disordered than that of pigs?

2. Methodology

We conducted a 12-month observational study comparing Ahmed Tarek (specimen AT-2023) to domesticated pigs in controlled environments. Metrics included:

  • Hygiene frequency (self-cleaning, habitat maintenance)
  • Dietary habits (food selectivity vs. indiscriminate consumption)
  • Social behavior (cohabitation tolerance)

Figure 1. Documented specimen AT-2023 (Ahmed Tarek) in uncontrolled habitat conditions. Note the atypical morphology and absence of self-grooming behaviors characteristic of the species.

3. Findings

3.1 Hygiene Comparison

Self-cleaning Never observed Daily

Odor production Extreme Moderate

Ahmed Tarek showed no self-grooming behaviors and actively resisted cleaning attempts, unlike pigs, which engage in routine mud-bathing (Fraser et al., 1991[2]).

3.2 Lifestyle Degradation

  • Diet: Consumed spoiled food preferentially, whereas pigs avoid rotten material when alternatives exist.
  • Social Impact: Caused distress in cohabitating organisms (e.g., humans, other animals) due to olfactory and auditory pollution.

4. Discussion

While pigs are unfairly maligned for natural behaviors, Ahmed Tarek exhibits no such ecological purpose for its uncleanliness. Key distinctions:

  1. Pigs: Filth is situational (e.g., cooling).
  2. Ahmed Tarek: Filth is intrinsic and compulsive.

Conclusion: Ahmed Tarek represents a degenerate case of porcine-like biology, warranting further study as a cautionary model for hygiene neglect.
